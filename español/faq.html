Frequently Asked Questions

During the three year program you will learn how to design and develop your own unique websites. The first year is focused on getting the basics down; learning HTML, CSS, and frameworks like Bootstrap to make sure your websites look good on any device. Junior year is when you get into JavaScript, which is a more complex programming language. Utilizing JavaScript in your website will allow you to include more complex features, like interactivity, animations, and more!

Your final year in the Web Design pathway program is basically a college course. We use NJIT's IS219 course curriculum and that means you can even receive college credit for satisfactorily completing the course. The curriculum includes more intense JavaScript projects, a Structured Learning Experience, and Vue.js.

After completing all three of the web design courses, you will be confident in your front-end development skills, and ready to continue your education in technology fields, or use your new invaluable knowledge as an incentive to colleges and/or future employers.

While we may not be creating video games during the school year, you will definitely be learning the languages and skills needed to create games.

If you Google 'why learn web development', you will find countless articles listing all of the positive things that come with learning web development. Devmountain Blog has a great list, including job availability, access to free educational resources, ability to work remotely, freelancing opportunities, and fostering your creativity. They do a much better job reviewing these points, so be sure to check out their article here: 7 Reasons You Should Learn Web Development

Web Designers are in high demand. Most businesses, large and small, have websites for their products and services. Web Designers can specialize in areas such as UX Designer, Multimedia Specialist, Application Developer, and Web-Content Manager.

If you are looking to be a web designer, there are a variety of college degree programs that you could pursue. Computer Science, Information Technology, Web & Information System, or Graphics Design are a few possible choices.

Web Design refers to the aesthetics and usability of a website. Web Designers decide on layouts, color schemes, graphics, and ease of access. Web Development on the other hand, is the functional portion of the web page, which focuses on bringing the design files to life.

The Web Design CTE Program consists of 1 course, each year, for 3 years. You will also receive your 21st Century Elective Credit. It is suggested that you remain in the program for its duration. You will have ample opportunities to take additional electives classes as well.
